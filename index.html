"Speedrunning chose me." - Niftski

"I am Niftski, a speedrunner from the United States. I speedrun Super Mario Bros for the NES. I hold multiple world records in numerous categories for this game."

[WORLD RECORD] Super Mario Bros. Any% - 4:54.948
Super Mario Bros. is one of the most culturally significant games ever, spawning a franchise lasting nearly 40 years. The Any% speedrun of this is equally iconic in speedrunning circles, with the simple goal of beating this legendary game as fast as possible.

This short, five minute run features a multitude of tricks that require many frame perfect inputs in quick succession, and was the first ever Super Mario Bros. Any% speedrun to come within one second of the theoretically perfect time, 4:54.03.
[WORLD RECORD] Super Mario Bros. Warpless - 18:56.911
The Warpless category of Super Mario Bros. is notoriously difficult to speedrun at the highest level - you need to play in a state of perfection for nearly 20 minutes straight, with absolutely no mistakes.

This run is the first ever 18:56 in this category, dethroning the previous record of 18:57.610 set by Niftski merely 5 days prior. This is Niftski's third world record in the Warpless category, and he plans to lower it by at least another second in the coming months.
[WORLD RECORD] Super Mario Bros. Minus World Ending - 2:30.802
The "Minus World" is one of gaming's most famous glitches: by clipping through a wall and entering the World 4 warp pipe from a different position than usual, the game sends you to an unintentional newly created world, generated from obscure pieces of the game's code. When this glitch is performed on the Japanese version of the game, the player is taken to a completable world with three remixed levels.

This run is the first ever 2:30 in this category, being the only run to take advantage of an incredibly precise clip in level -2 that can save up to 1.4 seconds. Niftski's run is nearly a second ahead of any other competition, and he plans to widen the gap even more in the future.
[WORLD RECORD] Super Mario Bros. 8-4 IL - 44.244
8-4 Individual Level, or "IL", speedruns are some of the most brutally optimised runs in Super Mario Bros., featuring many tricks called "fast accelerations". These quick backwards jumps at the start of a room give Mario a tiny speed boost each time they're performed, but they are incredibly hard to do perfectly, requiring 4 frame perfect inputs each time they're performed.

Niftski's current personal best in 8-4 IL tied the world record set less than 24 hours earlier by the Brazilian runner, LeKukie, and features eight perfect fast accels, along with multiple other frame perfect inputs. This record is only improvable by one frame, even theoretically, so it is safe to say that this is one of the hardest times to beat in all of Super Mario Bros.
[WORLD RECORD] Super Mario Bros.: The Lost Levels Any% - 7:52.721
Super Mario Bros. The Lost Levels was famously not sold in the West due to being perceived as "too hard" for the average gamer. This didn't stop Niftski, who was able to beat the entire game in under eight minutes. Whatsmore, this only took him one day to achieve, after setting a now-former world record in this game the day before.

Niftski's record features sublime play with many tough tricks performed back to back, leading to a gap of over a full second between him and second place.
[WORLD RECORD] Super Mario Bros. Any% No Coins - 4:59.889
Super Mario Bros. encourages you to gather coins wherever you go, and puts them in places that seem almost unavoidable. Despite this, Niftski is still able to beat the entire game without collecting a single coin in under 5 minutes.

This run features multiple unique pieces of movement to avoid collecting coins, such as a very tricky walljump in 8-1, where your jump has to be just low enough to avoid touching a coin while not falling beneath the level. This is the first, and so far only time under five minutes in this category.
